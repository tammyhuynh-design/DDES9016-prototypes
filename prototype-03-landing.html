Context

Design Brief

These prototypes explore how surfacing real-time purchase data — cost, carbon, cumulative spend — at different temporal scales can shift consumer behaviour at the point of decision. Grounded in Fogg's Behaviour Model (2009) and the value-action gap.

Three Scales

Checkout (transaction), Session (browse session), and Monthly (aggregate) represent three distinct temporal lenses on consumption — each triggering different cognitive responses and requiring different design responses.

Theoretical Frame

Fogg's motivation–ability–trigger triad structures each intervention. Damasio's somatic marker theory informs the emotional design layer. SDG 12 (responsible consumption) provides the overarching purpose frame.

Course Context

DDES9010 postgraduate design research, UNSW Art & Design. Assessment Task 01/02. Human-centred and speculative design methodology.
